There can be 4 different layouts of cards:

1. Horizontal -> all numbers on same line
This one is super-easy to read, not much complexity here

2. Vertical -> 4 rows, each row has 1 chunk of 4 digits, i.e.
xxxx
xxxx
xxxx
xxxx

3. Grid cards -> 2 rows, each row has 2 chunks of 4 digits each
xxxx xxxx
xxxx xxxx

During OCR we get raw texs like:
1. """
Federal Bank
40251
Issued by
8L001 D354by
 scopia
 8548
 Designed
 10/30
 DATE
"""
correct answer: "4025 8600 0354 8548"
How: pick up the potential card number details first
"""
40251
8L001 D354by
8548
10/30
"""

then, build-up the card number:
For above example we know that "40251" is the start of the card number
and since all the card numbers are NOT present in a single line, we know that it's not a horizontal
case. So, it can be either vertical or grid case.
Now, we try to find which case is it ?
from the potential card number details we see that there're 4 lines of card number chunks
are captured, so it's a vertical case.
Now, we try to build-up the card number:
Also, for a vertical card it can be wrapped by side '|' or '1's.
So, if all the chunks OR marjority of chunks contain '1', '|' at the starting or ending
it's very high probability that we need to remove those first.

So initial chunks will be:
"""
40251
8l001 D354by
5488
"""
we see that 4 chunks are : "40251", "8l001", "D354" and "5488"
Now, we need to build-up the card number from these chunks.
1st and 2nd chunk have 1 at their end, so very high probability that this '1' is just
'|' in that UI of physical card, so we remove that, then 
4 chunks become : "4025", "8l00", "D354" and "5488"
Now, for alphabets, we find closest substitution of that character.
After doing this:
chunks become: "4025", "8600", "0354" and "5488"
so final card number becomes: "4025 8600 0354 5488"
